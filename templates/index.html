    Desdemona in Othello

    Intent

    In reading Othello, the tragedy of Desdemona affected me deeply. She was a strong and caring woman who advocated ceaselessly for others and herself, yet was killed after Othello's jealousy sent him into a violent rage.

    The narrative of Desdemona as a silent victim or a "weak female character" is reductive and unfair. I made this website to analyze Desdemona's character in Othello in as many angles as possible, through the lens of art and life, as a woman who had a strong life and voice up until the very end.

    This kind of misconstruing of character matters because when stories about violence against women are perceived in a way that blames women for not being strong or proactive enough, it creates a dangerous narrative that affects the way that society perceives real victims of gender based violence.
